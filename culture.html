Culture & Travel

Culture as Research

Having traveled to nearly 30 countries, I approach travel not as tourism but as visual and cultural research. I observe hospitality rituals, table traditions, material craft, and social dining behaviors — translating them into contemporary luxury table concepts.

Each destination becomes a source of reference: the weight of a Portuguese ceramic, the geometry of Moroccan metalwork, the hierarchy of a formal Nordic table. These observations inform how I think about material, space, and guest experience.

Research Themes

◌

Hospitality Rituals

How cultures welcome, seat, and serve — from Japanese omotenashi to Middle Eastern generosity.

◌

Ceramic & Glass Craft

Traditional vessel-making traditions and how material culture informs contemporary luxury object design.

◌

Textile & Linen Traditions

The language of cloth on the table — from Portuguese embroidery to Scandinavian natural weaves.

◌

Shared Dining Formats

Meze, family-style, tasting sequence, mezze — how serving rituals shape the social experience of a meal.

◌

Color Languages

Regional color intuitions — the contrast palette of Andalusia, the muted tones of Northern Europe, the richness of South Asia.

◌

Spatial & Table Etiquette

The unspoken rules of table proximity, silence, eye contact, and service choreography across cultures.
